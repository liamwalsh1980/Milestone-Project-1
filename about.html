Our Autism Journey !

In 2014 my Wife and I had a Son. We both released that our Son's development had stopped when he was around 18 months of age. We decided to speak with our local GP who referred our Son for occupational therapy, physical therapy and speech & language tests. After many appointments over an 18 month period and just before our Son turned 3 years of age, we received a formal diagnosis that he was Autistic. We were both shocked but at the same time prepared for the fact that our lives were going to change forever. We took the decision from that point to Embrace Autism and accept the challenges ahead.

It's estimated that around 700,000 people in the UK have a diagnosis of autism with 1 in 100 children in the UK have a diagnosis of autism spectrum disorder.

Source: bma.org.uk updated September 2020

As a visitor here, you will feel part of a large and fantastic community.

What Worked For Us!

Take your child out everywhere.

I’m serious. It may be small trips at first but it is the best thing you can do. You’re teaching them coping skills. Life happens. People have to food shop, go to the bank, post office, etc. You would be doing it anyway if your kid didn’t have autism. Know their limits of course. Baby steps first. Today, it’s a trip to buy milk. Another time, maybe it’s a trip to the pet store and the library. Life from now on will take military-like planning. Warn them what’s coming, but go about your business. They smell fear. Seriously, the more panicked you are, they will turn that dial to “11.”
